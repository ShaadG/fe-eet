What this represents

A clean sine wave is the ideal output of an AC signal source — a function generator, an audio amplifier output, or the secondary side of a transformer. On a real oscilloscope this waveform should appear smooth with no jagged edges, no noise riding on top, and a consistent amplitude cycle to cycle.

  • Period calculation: One full cycle takes 2 divisions × 0.5 ms/div = 1 ms
  • Frequency: f = 1/T = 1/0.001 = 1,000 Hz = 1 kHz
  • Amplitude: 3 divisions peak × 2 V/div = 3 V (6 V peak-to-peak)
  • Vrms: Vpeak / √2 = 3 / 1.414 ≈ 2.12 V

  • How to isolate the noise: Switch the scope to AC coupling — this blocks the DC/low-frequency component and lets you zoom in on the noise layer alone.
  • Likely causes: Faulty bypass cap (high ESR), switching regulator interference, crosstalk from adjacent PCB trace.
  • Next step: Reduce V/div to 100–200 mV range in AC coupling mode to measure noise amplitude precisely, then cross-reference with the component's datasheet noise tolerance spec.

What this represents

A 3.3V CMOS logic signal — the kind you would see on a clock line, GPIO toggle, SPI chip select, or PWM output. The waveform transitions sharply between LOW (0V) and HIGH (3.3V). On an ideal scope trace the transitions appear nearly vertical; in practice they have a finite rise time and fall time (the time to transition from 10% to 90% of the voltage swing).

  • VOH (output high): 3.3V — must exceed the receiving device's minimum VIH to register as logic "1"
  • VOL (output low): 0V — must be below the receiving device's maximum VIL to register as logic "0"
  • Duty cycle: High time / Period × 100% = 0.5 ms / 1.0 ms × 100% = 50%
  • Rise time measurement: Zoom to 50 ns/div and measure the transition from 10% (0.33V) to 90% (2.97V) of the voltage swing
Common defects visible on a square wave:
• Ringing on edges — trace inductance + capacitive load, check impedance matching
• Slow rise time — excessive capacitive loading or weak driver, compare to datasheet max
• Reduced VOH — supply voltage sag, check power rail (connects back to Waveform 1/2 analysis)
• Wrong duty cycle — firmware configuration error or missing pull-up resistor
