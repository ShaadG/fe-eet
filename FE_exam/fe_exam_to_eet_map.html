Electronics — Analog & Instrumentation
On the job: Understanding op-amp behavior explains why a sensor output rail is saturated. Reading a diode or transistor in a datasheet and knowing its operating region tells you whether a component failure is plausible before you replace it. The instrumentation sub-topic maps directly to every DMM and scope setup decision.
Op-Amps Instrumentation Diodes / Transistors Amplifiers Power Electronics

Topic 15 8–12 Questions
Digital Systems
On the job: Logic gates, timing diagrams, and state machines are what you see on a scope when debugging a digital interface. Race conditions appear as glitches on the scope. Flip-flop timing explains setup and hold violations. Boundary scan (JTAG) is digital systems theory applied directly to in-circuit testing.
Logic Gates Timing Diagrams Flip-Flops Boundary Scan / JTAG Boolean Logic State Machines

Topic 12 6–9 Questions
Control Systems
On the job: Every switching power supply is a closed-loop control system. Bode plots show whether the loop is stable — a power supply that rings or oscillates under load has a phase margin problem. Understanding open vs. closed-loop response explains why a regulator behaves differently under no-load vs. full-load conditions.
Bode Plots Closed-Loop Stability Block Diagrams Steady-State Error Settling Time

Topic 1 11–17 Questions
Mathematics — Trig, Complex Numbers, Phasors
On the job: Phasors and complex impedance are the math behind every AC measurement. Law of Sines applies to PCB geometry verification. Trig identities are what the scope is showing you when you read amplitude, period, and phase shift off a waveform.
Trig / Law of Sines Complex Numbers Phasors Linear Algebra Calculus

Topic 2 4–6 Questions
Probability & Statistics
On the job: ICT yield is a binomial distribution. DMM calibration drift follows a normal distribution. Process capability (Cpk) is applied statistics. Every yield report, SPC control chart, and calibration interval decision is built on this math.
Normal Distribution Binomial Probability Cpk / SPC Mean / Median / Mode Expected Value

Power Systems

Power Factor Voltage Regulation Three-Phase Transformers Real / Reactive Power

Electromagnetics
On the job: Controlled-impedance traces on RF PCBs require transmission line theory to design and verify. Reflections from impedance mismatches show up as ringing on the scope. IPC-2221 trace width rules and stackup impedance calculations are electromagnetic theory made practical.
Transmission Lines Impedance Matching Maxwell Equations Wave Propagation Electrostatics

Communications

Fourier Series / FFT AM / FM Concepts Multiplexing Digital Communications PCM

SPI / I2C / UART Bus Timing Microprocessors Memory Systems Interfacing

PPE and lockout/tagout procedures are non-negotiable in any manufacturing environment. The safety sub-section of this topic is directly applicable every day.
